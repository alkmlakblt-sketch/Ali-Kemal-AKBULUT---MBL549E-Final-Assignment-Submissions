Finding
Compact = less surface = less loss
Relative compactness describes how efficiently a building encloses its volume. A cube (X1≈1.0) has minimum surface area for its volume. Every notch, recess, or wing adds surface — and more heat exchange with the exterior.
RF importance: 50/100 — second only to glazing area.
Anomaly
Building 5 breaks the pattern
B5 (Long bar, compactness 0.79) records the highest heating load of all 7m buildings at 38.6 kWh/m² — despite higher compactness than B6. Its elongated form maximises exposure, showing compactness and form interact.
Form geometry: compactness alone does not capture the full story.
Design implication
Irregular forms carry an energy cost
L, T, Z and U forms (B3, B10, B11, B12) all have lower compactness than rectangular boxes. Every articulation of the envelope adds surface area — increasing both heating and cooling demand.
In Athens: every 0.1 drop in compactness adds ~4–6 kWh/m² heating load on average.
Parameters 03 & 04 — Height (X5) & Orientation (X6)
Taller = more heat loss.
Direction = irrelevant.

Dataset & Methodology
About this research.
The UCI Energy Efficiency dataset was created by Tsanas & Xifara (2012) through systematic building energy simulation. 768 residential buildings were modelled in Ecotect, varying 8 architectural parameters to predict heating and cooling loads. All buildings share the same volume (771.75 m³) in the Athens, Greece climate zone.
On glazing area values: HL and CL values shown throughout this visualization represent the mean across all five glazing distribution scenarios (uniform distribution + four cardinal-biased variants: N, E, S, W at 55%/15%/15%/15%) for each glazing ratio. Glazing distribution (X8) has RF importance 39/100 and was treated as a secondary variable. This approach follows the aggregation method used in Tsanas & Xifara (2012) for inter-form comparison.

Building characteristics
Elementary cube construction
All 12 forms composed of 18 elementary cubes (3.5×3.5×3.5m), constant volume 771.75 m³. Materials: walls U=1.78, floors U=0.86, roofs U=0.50, windows U=2.26 W/(m²K).
Simulation context
Athens climate, residential use
7 persons, sedentary (70W), thermostat 19–24°C, clothing 0.6 clo, humidity 60%. Operation: 15–20h weekdays, 10–20h weekends. Infiltration: 0.5 ACH.
Limitations
Simulation ≠ field measurement
Ecotect simulation for Athens climate only. Volume held constant. Results may differ for other climates or occupancy patterns.
